✈️ Mach Loop Guide

The Mach Loop (or Machynlleth Loop) is a series of valleys in west-central Wales used by military aircraft for low-level training exercises. Situated between Dolgellau and Machynlleth, it offers rare opportunities to see fast jets flying below eye level — an unforgettable experience for aviation enthusiasts.

📅 RAF Flying Timetables

The Ministry of Defence publishes weekly updates on low flying schedules in the UK’s Tactical Training Areas, including LFA7, which covers the Mach Loop. While flight times aren't guaranteed, they provide a helpful guide.

📻 Radio Frequencies

Enhance your visit with a radio scanner and tune in to these common frequencies:

  • 130.490 MHz – VHF Low Level Common
  • 278.000 MHz – NATO Low Level
  • 280.350 MHz – West ICF
  • 128.700 MHz – London Military Central
  • 133.900 MHz – London Military West
  • 292.525 MHz – Lichfield Radar Corridor
  • 252.875 MHz – Daventry Radar Corridor

🛡️ Visitor Tips

  • Stay Safe: Weather can change quickly — bring proper gear and keep your footing secure.
  • Respect the Environment: Stick to paths, pack out all litter, and avoid disturbing wildlife or livestock.
  • Drone Restrictions: Drones are not permitted due to active military flight zones.
